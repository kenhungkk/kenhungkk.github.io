{\bf MATH 110 Linear Algebra}

\begin{enumerate}
\item {\em (Axler 7.B.6; clarified)} In finite dimension, prove that a normal operator on a complex inner product space is self-adjoint if and only if all its eigenvalues are real.

\item {\em (Axler 7.B.14)} Suppose $U$ is a finite-dimensional real vector space and $T \in \cL(U)$. Prove that $U$ has a basis consisting of eigenvectors of $T$ if and only if there is an inner product on $U$ that makes $T$ into a self-adjoint operator.

\item {\em (Axler 7.C.7)} Suppose $T$ is a positive operator on $V$. Prove that $T$ is invertible if and only if
\[
	\langle Tv, v \rangle > 0
\]
for every $v \in V$ with $v \ne 0$.

\item {\em (Axler 7.C.9)} Prove or disprove: the identity operator on $\mathbb{F}^2$ has infinitely many self-adjoint square roots.

\item {\em (Axler 7.D.2)} Give an example of $T \in \cL(\CC^2)$ such that $0$ is the only eigenvalue of $T$ and the singular values of $T$ are $5, 0$.

\end{enumerate}
