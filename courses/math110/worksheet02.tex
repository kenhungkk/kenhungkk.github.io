\item {\em True or False?} Suppose $V$ is a vector space over a field $\mathbb{F}$. The additive inverse must be unique.

\item {\em (Axler 1.B.3)} Suppose $v, w \in V$. Explain why there exists a unique $x \in V$ such that $v + 3x = w$.

\item {\em (Axler 1.B.6)} Let $\infty$ and $-\infty$ denote two distinct object, neither of which is in $\RR$. Define an addition and scalar multiplication on $\RR \cup \{\infty\} \cup \{-\infty\}$ as you could guess from the notation. Specifically, the sum and product of two real numbers is as usual, and for $t \in \RR$ define
\[
	t \infty = \begin{cases}
		-\infty & \text{if } t < 0, \\
		0 & \text{if } t = 0, \\
		\infty & \text{if } t > 0,
	\end{cases} \qquad t(-\infty) = \begin{cases}
		\infty & \text{if } t < 0, \\
		0 & \text{if } t = 0, \\
		-\infty & \text{if } t > 0,
	\end{cases}
\]
and
\begin{align*}
	t + \infty &= \infty + t = \infty, \\
	t + (-\infty) &= (-\infty) + t = -\infty \\
	\infty + \infty & = \infty, \\
	(-\infty) + (-\infty) &= -\infty, \\
	\infty + (-\infty) &= 0.
\end{align*}
Is $\RR \cup \{\infty\} \cup \{-\infty\}$ a vector space over $\RR$? Explain.

\item Suppose $V$ (and $W$) is the subset consisting of differentiable (twice-differentiable) functions in $\RR^{[0, 1]}$, respectively. Is $W$ a subspace in $V$?

\item {\em (Axler 1.C.11)} Prove that the intersection of two subspaces of $V$ is a subspace of $V$.

\item {\em (Axler 1.C.12)} Prove that the union of two subspaces of $V$ is a subspace of $V$ if and only if one of the subspaces is contained in the other.
\end{enumerate}
